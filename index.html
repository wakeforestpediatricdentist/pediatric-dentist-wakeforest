Comprehensive Services by a Pediatric Dentist in Wake Forest

We offer a complete range of pediatric dental services under one roof. Whether your child is due for a routine cleaning or needs emergency care, our team is equipped with the tools, training, and compassion to help.

Pediatric Preventive Dentistry

Prevention is key to maintaining long-term oral health. Our preventive services include routine dental exams, professional cleanings, digital X-rays, fluoride treatments, and sealants. These services are essential for identifying issues early and protecting teeth from cavities and gum disease.

Pediatric Restorative Dentistry

When dental problems do occur, our restorative treatments are designed to preserve your child’s natural smile. We offer tooth-colored fillings, crowns, and space maintainers. Each treatment is tailored to be as minimally invasive as possible, prioritizing your child’s comfort and recovery.

Special Needs Pediatric Dentistry

We proudly provide inclusive care for children with physical, developmental, behavioral, or sensory needs. Our staff has experience and training in adapting dental visits to suit each child’s comfort level. Whether it’s using alternative communication, longer appointment times, or sedation dentistry, our team creates a safe, respectful experience for every child.

Emergency Pediatric Dentistry

Dental emergencies can happen unexpectedly—from broken teeth to infections and severe pain. Our Wake Forest office offers same-day emergency appointments to address urgent dental needs. Prompt care not only eases discomfort but also helps prevent more serious complications.

Pediatric Sedation Dentistry

For children with dental anxiety or those requiring complex procedures, sedation dentistry is a safe and effective solution. We offer multiple sedation options, including nitrous oxide and oral conscious sedation, to help ensure a calm, pain-free experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the best age for a child’s first dental visit?
A: According to the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry, children should visit a dentist by age one or within six months of the first tooth appearing.

A: Yes. We provide comprehensive Special Needs Pediatric Dentistry, ensuring personalized care for children with a wide range of developmental or medical conditions.

Q: Are emergency appointments available?
